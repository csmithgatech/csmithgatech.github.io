A Milestone Year in the Making

Dear Brown alumni,

A small group of trailblazers walked onto campus in October 1891 and into a new chapter of the University’s history. The 2021-22 academic year marks 130 years since the first women attended Brown and 120 years since the first women of color began their studies. The Brown Women’s Network will commemorate these milestones with events throughout the year to help Brown women forge connections, celebrate their achievements, and reflect on common experiences.

This fall we also welcome the next generation of changemakers to campus, a group selected from the largest applicant pool in the University’s history. Join us on September 6 for the live streamed Class of 2025 convocation.

How will the coming years help shape these future alumni? How will they contribute to the meaningful work and creative problem solving that our worldwide Brown community is known for? What an exciting time to be a Brunonian.

Be well and stay Ever True,

Andy Shaindlin ’86, Vice President for Alumni Relations
Carlos Lejnieks ’00, President, Brown Alumni Association

#GoBruno in Tokyo!

Did you catch the Olympics earlier this month? So did we, and we were cheering on (and about) the eight Brunonians who were in Tokyo.

Barbershop Work-ups, Courtesy of Marshala Lee MD’11

Dr. Lee has committed her career to addressing health disparities—including creating a program that brings preventative screenings to local barber shops—and increasing the number of doctors from underrepresented backgrounds.

Carlos Lejnieks ’00 believes in the power of mentorship. The first Latinx president of the Brown Alumni Association hopes to grow mentoring for historically underrepresented groups during his term, which began July 1.

Dr. Shideh Shafie MD’07 offers tips for practicing self-advocacy in healthcare so you won’t find yourself tongue-tied at the doctor’s office.

Rachel Boynton ’95 explores questions of national memory in her upcoming documentary “Civil War (Or, Who Do We Think We Are).”

“Love The World” by DAP The Contract (Dolapo Akinkugbe ’16) was chosen for NPR’s 2021 Tiny Desk Top Shelf series.

Seven new graduates discuss their on-campus academic experience with the Brown Alumni Magazine.

Yanling He EMCS ’21 has been named a finalist in the 2021 Cyber Security Woman of the Year Awards.

Sosie Bacon ’14 scores a starring role in HBO’s new hit crime drama “Mare of Easttown.”
